Influential Philosophers

Epicurus (341 - 270 BC)

Ancient Greek philosopher often misunderstood as an advocate of excess. In reality, he championed simple living, meaningful friendships, and the absence of pain as the greatest good.

Lucius Annaeus Seneca (4 BC - 65 AD)

Roman Stoic philosopher who emphasized virtue, resilience, and the pursuit of inner peace despite external hardships. His letters and treatises remain profoundly relevant to modern life.

Marco Aurelio (121 - 180 AD)

Ancient Roman emperor and a stoic philosopher. The last emperor of the Pax Romana, an age of relative peace, calm, and stability for the Roman Empire

Michel de Montaigne (1533 - 1592)

was one of the most significant philosophers of the French Renaissance. He is known for popularising the essay as a literary genre. His work is noted for its merging of casual anecdotes and autobiography with intellectual insight.

Ludwig Feuerbach (1804 - 1872)

German philosopher interested in the religious field and into the effects of alimentation.

Carl Jung (1875 - 1961)

Swiss psychologist often opposed to Sigmund Freud, focused on symbols, individual and collective inconscious power rather than childhood.
